Mount Etna

Mount Etna, located in Sicily, is Europe’s most active volcano and one of the world’s most studied ones. It’s in near-constant activity, with frequent eruptions that shape the landscape and fascinate scientists.

It’s also a UNESCO World Heritage site and has been erupting for over 500,000 years! Locals call it “Mongibello” (beautiful mountain), and it’s both feared and respected for its power and beauty.

Interesting fact :

Ancient Romans believed Mount Etna was the forging workshop of Vulcan, the god of fire and blacksmithing, and that giants were trapped beneath it!
