Mount Sinai

Mount Sinai is believed to be the place where Moses received the Ten Commandments from God, making it an important religious site for Jews, Christians, and Muslims. Thousands of pilgrims and tourists climb it every year, often at night to witness the beautiful sunrise.

The mountain is part of the Sinai Peninsula and stands at 2,285 meters. Despite its rugged terrain, the path to the summit is filled with ancient chapels and monasteries, giving it a deeply spiritual atmosphere.
